This is a demo version of mediaexperience.JS script: a media queries addition based on javascript.

mediaexperience.JS is like no-js for mediaqueries. Media queries are the future of course. The support is wide in most of the browsers except the old IEs.

Opt-out from responsice experience

I assume most of us never thought about disabling responsiveness when building a responsive website. But there are scenarios, where mobile experience is not sufficient but the client has no way to opt-out. It's just because you did not thought about it when writing your CSS.

Inspired by conditional classes of modernizr

You can watch any element for it's size and respond to it. You are not constrained to the screen size. You can however watch body for size and apply screen experience classes to the body element.

No-mediaqueries fall-back

As with no-js approach, you can hardcode the initial experience for any element. Try toggling button and try resizing the browser:
